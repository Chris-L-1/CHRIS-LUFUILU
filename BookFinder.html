BookFinder – Book Searching App

A mobile app that helps students and avid readers quickly find the original, legitimate version of a book, using either text search or a photo of the book cover.

Role UI/UX Designer
Duration 1 week (concept project)
Tools Figma
Type Mobile app design
View prototype in Figma

The Challenge

Students and readers often waste time on unreliable websites trying to find the right version of a book. Search results can be confusing, some copies are not legitimate, and users are never sure if they have found the correct, original edition.

The goal of BookFinder is to make it simple to search for a specific book and be redirected to a trusted retailer that offers the correct version.

User Research

To understand current behaviour, a small survey and informal interviews were done with students and frequent readers who search for books online and in apps. Key questions explored how often they read, how they currently search, and what frustrates them about existing tools.

  • Many users are not confident that the books they find online are original or unedited.
  • Most apps only support text search by title or author; people also want to search using the cover image.
  • Too many recommendations and ads make it hard to know which source is trustworthy.

Persona & Needs

The primary persona is Peter, a 24‑year‑old student who reads often and looks for books that he cannot find in the library. He wants a simple, affordable way to find legitimate copies online without wasting time.

  • Easy‑to‑use app with a clear search flow.
  • Ability to search by typing the name, author, ISBN, or by uploading a cover photo.
  • Confidence that links lead to trusted retailers and not misleading websites.

User Journey & Flow

The journey focuses on a quick path from opening the app to landing on a trusted retailer page. Two main search methods were designed: manual text search and image search using a photo of the book cover.

  • Home: one main search bar for title / author / ISBN and a secondary button for image search.
  • Search: results list with book cards that show cover, title, author, and a short description.
  • Book details: full information and a primary button “Find the book online” that opens a trusted retailer.

Design Process

The design started as simple dot‑style wireframes to define the hierarchy on each screen. From there, high‑fidelity mockups were created in Figma, focusing on clarity, trust and consistency across the flow.

Wireframes

Early wireframes explored different ways of combining text search, image search and trending titles on the home screen. The final structure uses one main search bar, a clear “Image search” button, and a horizontal carousel of trending books.

High‑Fidelity Screens

The final UI uses a green brand color, card‑based layouts and strong typographic hierarchy. Each screen highlights the main action: searching, choosing a result, or tapping the “Find the book online” button.

Final Solution

BookFinder gives users two simple ways to find a book: typing the title, author or ISBN, or using an image of the cover. Once a book is selected, the details screen confirms the edition and then sends the user to a trusted retailer with a single tap.

Key Learnings & Next Steps

This project showed how important trust and clarity are when designing for online purchases. Clearly separating search methods and explaining where links lead helps users feel more confident.

Next steps would include usability testing with students, adding more retailers, and exploring features like user voting on the best source for each book.
